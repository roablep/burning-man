# Module 7: Youth Voice — Language, Values & Sentiment by Age

**Research Question:** How does Under-30 language, values, and emotional tone differ from older cohorts?

**Methodology:** Semantic classification of 1173 Q5 responses (2024+2025, all survey types) segmented by age group. Sample: Full Dataset.

## Top 10 Themes (Overall)
- **Burning Man:** 76
- **Self Expression:** 31
- **Emotional Spectrum:** 17
- **Self Discovery:** 13
- **Burning Man Experience:** 10
- **Mixed Emotions:** 7
- **Cultural Identity:** 6
- **Self Acceptance:** 6
- **Human Connection:** 5
- **Openness:** 5

## Top Themes by Age Group
- **Under 30** (n=199): Burning Man, Self Expression, Emotional Spectrum, Mixed Emotions, First Time
- **30-39** (n=479): Burning Man, Self Expression, Emotional Spectrum, Burning Man Experience, Cultural Identity
- **40-49** (n=222): Burning Man, Self Expression, Self Discovery, Emotional Spectrum, Mixed Emotions
- **50+** (n=232): Burning Man, Self Discovery, Self Expression, Human Connection, Affirmative Response

> Under-30 voices emphasize **Mixed Emotions, First Time** — themes largely absent from older cohorts. Older cohorts lean toward **Burning Man Experience, Self Discovery**. Values language diverges: young respondents (n=199) most often express **creativity**, while older cohorts foreground **community**.
