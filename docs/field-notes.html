Field Notes Analysis

Thematic analysis of 2024–2025 participant narratives · mixed-method · LLM-assisted coding

154 transformation narratives analyzed 8.4% mention physical hardship 18.2% experienced new love on playa 6 thematic modules

What We Studied

Handwritten field notes from 2024 and 2025 were OCR'd using Google Gemini, then analyzed across six thematic modules using semantic classification and LLM-based coding. Each module asks a specific research question about the participant experience.

"from the 1st time I heard of it about 15 yr ago, so yeah... a calling that changed me and now that I'm here, MAKES SO MUCH SENSE — I need time to ponder on how it has changed me because I'm still navigating my 1st BURN — BUT I feel home partying with you all..."
— First-time participant, 2024

Six Modules at a Glance

Module 1

The Pilgrim's Progress

How does transformation evolve from Virgin to Veteran?

Virgins prioritize Anticipation and Acceptance; Elders shift toward Community and Connection. The I:We linguistic ratio stays stable across tenure — transformation is real but doesn't mean ego death.

Virgin I:We ratio 8.4 Elder I:We ratio 7.5 1 shared top theme
Module 2

The Ordeal as Catalyst

Is physical hardship what drives transformation?

No — only 8.4% of transformation narratives mention hardship at all (vs. 8.8% baseline). Participants describe transformation through social and creative lenses, not physical survival. The Ordeal Hypothesis is largely unsupported.

8.4% mention hardship 3.1% cite it as catalyst Ordeal Hypothesis: weak
Module 3

Mask vs. Mirror

Do costumes hide identity or reveal it?

Costume is a Mirror, not a Mask. Play is the dominant motivation (46.5%), with Authenticity second (22.8%). Disguise — the "mask" motive — was coded at exactly 0% across all age groups.

Play: 46.5% Authenticity: 22.8% Disguise: 0%
Module 4

Sacred vs. Profane

How do the Man and Temple differ emotionally?

The Temple is a space of grief (13.6% vs. 3.0% for the Man). The Man inspires Indifference as its most common emotion — a possible routinization of the symbol. Men report Temple grief at higher rates than women (16.1% vs. 10.6%).

Temple grief: 13.6% Man's top emotion: Indifference Gender gap in Temple grief
Module 5

The Other in Utopia

How does the marginalized experience compare to Radical Inclusion ideals?

Among those who opted into the Diversity survey, 82.3% reported Negative or Mixed identity impacts — but this reflects self-selection, not a general population rate. In baseline surveys, marginalized groups reported friction at rates similar to the majority (21.4% vs. 22.8%).

82.3% Negative/Mixed (survey opt-in) Marginalized friction: 21.4% Majority friction: 22.8%
Module 6

Playa Love vs. Default Love

How do relationship dynamics differ in the temporary autonomous zone?

Only 18.2% experienced new romantic connection on playa. Most committed relationships saw No Change or ambiguous outcomes — but the dominant themes in playa relationships are Openness, Authenticity, and Intensity compared to default-world norms.

18.2% new love Top themes: Openness, Intensity 137 responses
Methodology note: Analysis uses LLM-based semantic coding of handwritten field notes (OCR'd via Google Gemini). Sample sizes vary by module (62–154 responses). The 2025 dataset is substantially smaller than 2024 — avoid unweighted cross-year comparisons. Module 5 figures are subject to self-selection bias as noted above.
