Census Analysis

2025 weighted census · 5,028 respondents · cohort years 1986–2024

+0.013 retention trend slope per cohort year 11.5% of current respondents are under 30 +0.67 camp placement retention gap for ≤22 year-olds

Key Findings

Retention trend

Overall second-year return rates show a modest upward trend of +0.013 per cohort year across 34 cohort years — the community is slowly getting stickier.

Youth composition

11.5% of 2025 census respondents are under 30. The chart below shows how many current under-30s fall into each entry cohort — but because early cohort members have aged past 30 by now, this cannot tell us whether youth recruitment has improved over time.

Camp placement effect

Being in a placed camp strongly predicts return for the youngest burners: a +0.67 retention gap for ≤22 year-olds (placed vs. unplaced). Effect diminishes with age.

Methodology note: Many cells — especially for younger age bands in earlier cohort years — have fewer than 30 weighted respondents. Treat single-cohort figures for ≤22 and 23–28 age bands with caution. Global trend lines are more reliable than cell-level values.

Three Key Charts

1. Under-30 members by entry cohort

Share of each cohort's 2025 respondents who are currently under 30, split by camp placement. Caveat: early cohort members have simply aged past 30 — the near-zero values before 2018 reflect aging, not low youth attendance in those years. This chart shows where today's young burners entered, not a trend in recruitment.

2. Who comes back?

Second-year return rates by age band. Use the dropdown to filter by cohort era (All, 2015–2018, 2022–2024).

3. Does camp placement matter?

Retention gap (camp-placed minus unplaced) by age band over the last 10 cohort years. Positive = placed burners return more. The ≤22 gap of +0.67 is striking but based on small samples.
